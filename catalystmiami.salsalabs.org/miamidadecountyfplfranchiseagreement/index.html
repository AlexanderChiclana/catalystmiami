What is a franchise agreement?

Miami-Dade County residents pay an additional $27 million in fees every year on their electricity bills to Florida Power & Light, passed through to the County. The current contract was negotiated in 1989 for the privilege of placing electrical equipment on County property. County residents have paid hundreds of millions of dollars in franchise fees during the last 30 years.

Why should we negotiate?

FPL is keeping Miami-Dade  in the past, while other states transition to an efficient, clean, and more economically competitive 21st century economy. Florida is the Sunshine State with the 3rd-most solar potential in the nation, but FPL opposed greater access to solar in a deceptive ballot initiative in 2016. We use the most air conditioning in the nation, but FPL invests the least in the nation in low-income energy efficiency programs . We experience the most hurricane landfalls in the nation, but in 2017 most of Miami-Dade lost power for nearly a week due to Hurricane Irma, a Category 1 storm, due to FPL’s lackluster response.

Miamians for Energy Freedom imagines a 21st century energy future for Miami-Dade County. A future where solar power is easily accessible; where our utility expands, instead of  reduces money saving efficiency programs; where our disaster resilience is regularly assessed and adaptations are implemented; and where our utility serves its mission to work for its customers, not just its shareholders.

You have the power.

In the next year and a half, the County will have the opportunity to renegotiate our contract, also known as a franchise agreement, with FPL. You have the right to vote on our County’s contract with FPL. Join the Miami Climate Alliance and Miamians for Energy Freedom in urging elected leaders to give voters an agreement that builds a more resilient and renewable energy future.

It's time we get our money's worth through the County’s contract with FPL
This email will be delivered to
Subject: It's time we get our money's worth through the FPL franchise agreement
Message:
We all know that Florida is the sunshine state. However, our state's monopoly utilities are upholding barriers that keep us stuck in the past: we lag behind smaller, cloudier regions in terms of solar; data needed by County staff to move our region towards a clean energy future is not shared; energy efficiency programs available to our most under-resourced community members have the lowest investment in the nation; despite billions spent on hardening our power grid, last year much of our County lost power for several days due to a category 1 hurricane.

Every year, Miami-Dade County residents pay an additional $27 million on their electricity bills to Florida Power & Light, which then gets passed through to the County. This pass-through tax was set up in 1989 through the County's franchise agreement with FPL. Through this agreement, FPL gains the right to use County property to deliver electricity. Coming up within the next year and a half, the County will have the opportunity to renegotiate this agreement.

Without a doubt, it's time to move beyond the status quo and give Miami-Dade County the freedom to move towards a more resilient and renewable energy future. Especially, since our County made the commitment to be one of the world's 100 Resilient Cities. I ask that you use your influence to ensure that the renewed franchise agreement provides significant environmental, economic, and community benefits to Miami-Dade County. In particular, I would like to see the following:

- Aggressive renewable energy goals, such as 100% of our electricity coming from renewable sources
- Construction of a community solar installation in Miami-Dade County that provides a direct benefit to working class and low-income FPL customers in the County, with a specific goal of meaningfully reducing their energy burden
- Establish a partnership where our County's existing low-income weatherization programs are expanded to include energy efficiency and upgraded HVAC systems with the goal of serving a minimum of 1,000 households per year
- A collaborative annual review with MDC regarding infrastructural vulnerabilities to sea level rise, projected heat index, hurricanes, storm surge, and other climate considerations for the purpose of identifying and implementing necessary adaptations
- Limit the term of this franchise agreement to no more than 10 years in order to maintain both accountability and flexibility
- Dedicate the funds acquired by the County through the franchise fee to renewable energy initiatives, energy assistance, and/or community resiliency initiatives specific to post-hurricane energy access for vulnerable communities.

I appreciate your time and hope you will consider my request.
